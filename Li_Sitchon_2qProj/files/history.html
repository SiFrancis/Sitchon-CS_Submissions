OUR HISTORY

    HyperLink was founded in 1994 by Christopher Snow, a Professor of Computer Science at Caltech, together with fellow professors and experts in other fields such as law, sociology, and economics. He founded the organization after attending the 1994 Superhighway Summit, a national conference that addressed the societal implications of the Internet and advances in communications technology.

    Prof. Snow and his associates used their knowledge and connections to push greater Internet adoption and protection of digital rights. Of these efforts, there are several that you may have heard of:


PROJECT I-SPY

    Project I-Spy exposed harmful spyware present in more than 1000 apps distributed throughout India and Southeast Asia, which were used to facilitate expansive scam operations.

RAMIREZ V. UNITED STATES

    HyperLink played a huge role in the victory of the Ramirez v. United States case in 2009, which protected the rights of journalists who publish their works online.
